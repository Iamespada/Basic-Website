The Alaskan Malamute is a large breed of domestic dog (Canis lupus familiaris) originally bred for hauling heavy freight because of their strength and endurance, and later an Alaskan sled dog. They are similar to other arctic breeds, like the Greenland dog, Canadian Eskimo Dog, the Siberian Husky and the Samoyed.

Nicknames Mal or Mally
Country of Origin Alaska, United States of America
Traits
Weight Male 85 pounds
Female 75 pounds
Height Male 25 inches
Female 23 inches
Coat Thick, a double coat, with plush undercoat
Colour Gray, sable, black or red, always with white, as well as all white
Litter Size 4-10 puppies
Lifespan Up to 16 years
